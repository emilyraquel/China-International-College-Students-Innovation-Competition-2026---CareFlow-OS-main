Smart Triage for Pediatric Pneumonia

A lightweight cloud architecture designed to shield diagnoses from geographical variations and modernize community radiology where it is needed most.

Explore Technology View Global Challenge

The Equity Gap in Pediatric Care

Pediatric pneumonia is the leading cause of global mortality in children under 5. Resource disparities worsen this scenario.

Urban Centers

Full integration into PACS ecosystems and rapid access to radiologists, but triage teams still suffer from stress and cognitive fatigue under heavy bed overcrowding.

Remote Primary Care

High diagnostic variability. Rural and peripheral clinics face a shortage of 24/7 specialists, significantly delaying critical interventions.

Global Connection: Vulnerable regions and cold provinces (such as Heilongjiang) face harsh winters and severe seasonal respiratory spikes that frequently collapse healthcare frontlines.

Lightweight Cloud Processing & Seamless UX

Zero Hardware CapEx. End-to-end optimization without the need for expensive local infrastructure investments.

1

Ingestion Phase

The medical staff exports the X-ray directly from the PACS system into a secure, intuitive web interface.

2

Processing Phase

An algorithm shielded against geographic biases, X-ray intensities, and global equipment brand variations.

3

Result Phase

Real-time delivery of explanatory heatmaps and probability indexes straight to the medical triage team.

High Scalability

Social Impact & The Foundation of Equity

Three core pillars focused on public health sustainability and transformation.

01

Job Creation

Technical training for frontline nursing staff and new specialized positions in data engineering and local support for HealthTechs.

02

Health & Well-being

Direct, measurable reduction in childhood pneumonia mortality and mitigation of chronic stress/burnout among general medical staff.

03

Sustainability

Lightweight cloud processing eliminates the massive carbon footprint typically associated with heavy local servers and computing hardware.
